## The same-kind groups the text never declared

**A. The two family-gathering laws minted in this delta — INV-159 and INV-160.** Both gather a set of sibling
invariants under one home and state how the family holds. They gather in *opposite* postures: INV-160
enumerates its members, states a parity, and declares that a member naming no net is a class defect the
**prover blocks** [INV-125] — an enforced membership. INV-159 lists the same-role instances it absorbs
(T-16, INV-21, INV-29, INV-15, INV-41, A-3, INV-74, INV-75, INV-127, E-17, INV-103, INV-134, INV-156,
INV-160) as *cites*, with no clause making an uncited or stale-rooted forward-binding duty a prover-blockable
defect. Its only net is the one-time repoint check (M-306), not a standing guard on future members.

**B. The pack-ships-the-shape / host-owns-the-instantiation split — E-26, INV-125, INV-136, INV-139, against
the fully-centralized INV-158.** The delta's E-26 clause frames the per-project removal-scanner as "a
deliberate difference from the one-home browser harness [INV-158]" — contrasting against a single object. But
the reasoning it uses (the body is host-specific, so the pack ships only the shape and the host owns the
per-surface instantiation) is a recurring policy: INV-125 ships the rule + prover lens and leaves the
DOM-wide assertion to the host ("it has no DOM of its own"); INV-139 ships the law + default numbers + script
and leaves the browser-computed assertion to the adopting suite, "the split the interactive-overlap rule
takes [INV-136]." INV-158 is the outlier that fully centralizes *because* its core is host-agnostic. E-26
contrasts itself against the exception while sharing its role with an undeclared majority family.

## Parity check

For group A, the property that matters: does a new member's obligation to the family get *guarded*, or only
*conventionally cited*? INV-160 guards (prover blocks a netless member). INV-159 only cites — a new
"binds forward" clause that names a stale root, or none, is caught by no standing lens.

For group B, the property: what discriminates a centralize-fully member (INV-158) from a ship-the-shape
member (E-26/INV-125/INV-136/INV-139)? It is whether the pack can ship an *identical body* every host runs.
That discriminator is stated nowhere; E-26 re-derives it locally against one neighbour.

For INV-160's own flat parity ("each names the net that reddens a run on its own violation"): eight members
carry a mechanical run-failing check, but INV-77's "net" is a real-device walk row *the suite can never
green*, owed to the human's hands [kin INV-30] — red by construction, not reddening on a violation. The flat
sentence fits INV-77 only by stretch.

## The one question (finding 1), both objects in hand

**INV-160** (:657) closes its class with an enforced membership: *"a member naming no net is a class defect the
prover blocks [INV-125]."* A future suite-honesty invariant that forgets its net is caught mechanically.
**INV-159** (:340) gathers the forward-binding family — fourteen cited instances including the four
self-enforcing landing rules [INV-103, INV-134, INV-156, INV-160] — but states only that each duty *"cites
[it] in place of restating it."* No clause makes a new "binds forward" clause that cites a stale root, or none
at all, a defect any standing lens catches; the repoint test M-306 is a one-time sweep of today's cites, not a
guard on tomorrow's.

**Should INV-159 carry the same enforced membership INV-160 does — a standing check (extending M-306) that a
"binds forward" clause resolving to a root other than INV-159, or naming forward-binding with no cite, is a
prover-blockable defect — or is the forward-binding citation convention deliberately advisory, guarded once at
repoint and trusted thereafter?**

Recommended default: **give INV-159 the parallel guard.** Both laws exist to keep a family from drifting;
INV-160 got a future-member net and INV-159 did not, in the same delta, which reads as an oversight rather
than a decision. A standing lens — "a clause whose text says *binds forward* / *looks forward only* / *never
retroactively* must cite INV-159" — mirrors INV-160's [INV-125] guard and costs one test. The class sentence
lands only on the human's word [INV-142]; the lane is not held.
