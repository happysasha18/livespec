## The strongest finding (finding 1), both objects in hand

The row-369 lift raised the sub-domain-completeness duty to the general level of the range law and moved two of
its three skill homes to match: **INV-138** (:1779) now leads with "a guarantee scoped to a named part of its
domain draws the standing question about the remainder … a band of a ranged quantity, a user state, a network
condition, a locale, any named sub-case", and the **prover's named-part ask** (product-prover:269) leads with
the same general sentence, "its worked instance is the viewport". The design review's home did not move with
them. Its lens is still `## The standing viewport-band lens (SPEC INV-138)` (design-reviewer:60): the title,
the declared by-construction group ("the viewport bands … are a same-kind group, since one layout guarantee is
meant to hold the same element across every band"), and the whole worked body are viewport bands, and the lift
appended one sentence — "this over-bands lens runs the same duty a user state, a network condition, or a locale
would draw on its own group."

That appended sentence sharpens the gap rather than closing it. It asserts the other sub-domains draw the duty
"on their own group", but it gives them no by-construction lens: the pass constructs the viewport-band group by
name, and a guarantee scoped to logged-in with logged-out silent, or to one locale with the rest silent, has no
construction step — it falls to the bottom-up five steps, which fire only when two role sentences already
match. The by-construction lenses exist because a guarantee scoped to one part leaves the OTHER parts with no
named element for the bottom-up walk to catch: a caption law that speaks for "a phone" names no landscape-phone
element, so the walk has nothing to pair — which is exactly why viewport bands earned a by-construction lens in
the first place. A guarantee scoped to logged-in names no logged-out element for the same reason, so the same
blind spot the by-construction lens was built to cover now reopens for every sub-domain that is not the
viewport. And INV-138's clause routes it straight here: "Where the parts are a same-kind group no clause has
yet declared, the design review's group pass reaches them" — the law expects this pass to reach any undeclared
sub-domain group, not the viewport band alone.

**The question to the human:** how alike should the design review's group pass and the prover's named-part ask
behave over non-viewport sub-domains? Two resolutions are defensible, and the deciding fact is the design's
intent, not the text: (a) **generalize** the design-reviewer lens to a named-part group lens — the
by-construction group pass over any sub-domain a guarantee scopes to (viewport bands, user states, network
conditions, locales), with viewport bands kept inline as its worked group — mirroring the prover's named-part
ask and INV-138's general clause; or (b) **decide by intent** that the prover's grain is general because it
runs a mechanical sweep while the design review's lens stays concrete because it teaches a reviewer one vivid
group, the bottom-up walk deemed enough for the other sub-domains — recorded as a decided sentence so the
narrower grain reads as a decision, not an omission.

**Recommended default: (a), generalize the lens.** The prover already carries the general reach and INV-138
already sends undeclared sub-domain groups to this pass, so a user-state or locale guarantee with a silent
remainder is a blind spot the design review is on the hook for but has no by-construction step to catch. Keep
the viewport-band worked example inline for concreteness; the generalization is the lens's grain, the example
its illustration. This is raised as the pass's one ask rather than a flat recommendation because resolution (b)
is genuinely open — whether the design review OWES a by-construction lens for non-viewport sub-domains, or the
bottom-up walk suffices there, is a design-intent call the text does not settle. It rides the echo channel: the
shared role fits one plain sentence, the difference is a whole behaviour (by-construction reach over
non-viewport sub-domains) the prover carries and this pass lacks, and no spec sentence decides the difference
[INV-141 echo conditions].

## The instance-earning shape across the family — finding 2, both objects

Beside the grain question, the lift throws the family into relief: the pack now holds several "general law +
concrete instances" pairs, and they do not share one shape for **when a new instance earns its own spec
sentence versus rides the general silently**. **INV-138** (:1779) names ONE worked instance (the viewport) and
lets every other named part ride the general — a locale- or user-state- or network-scoped guarantee is caught
by the general duty with no instance sentence of its own, and earns a worked sentence only if a real incident
works it, the way the 2026-07-16 rotated-phone incident worked the viewport. **INV-41** (:1961) does the
opposite: it enumerates every per-kind budget instance in the clause. The **curated facet list** (:385)
enumerates every member and earns each a facet sentence by a named incident [INV-18]; **INV-126** (:1775) grew
its magnitude sub-question into the clause when a named incident arrived. So the family runs at least two
patterns — name-the-worked-instance-only (INV-138, and INV-126's other gestures) versus enumerate-every-member
(INV-41, the facet list) — with no declared shape saying which a given law should take.

The choice is not arbitrary: it tracks whether the member set is **open-ended** (INV-138's sub-domains: any
domain, any named sub-case, unbounded) or **closed and small** (INV-41's members: the project kinds, a finite
enumerable set). An open set can only name its worked instances and let the rest ride the general; a closed set
can enumerate. But that keying is declared nowhere — the composition-lens family [INV-125, INV-126, INV-136,
INV-138] groups these laws by cross-axis composition, and the curated-list class [INV-18] governs which members
a list may gain, and neither decides the enumerate-vs-ride-silently axis. **Recommended default:** a short
class sentence — a general law names its worked instances (each earned by incident [INV-18]) and lets an
open-ended member set ride the general silently, while a closed, enumerable member set names every member —
homed where the composition-lens family or the curated-list class is stated, so the next general law drafted
does not pick the shape by feel. The alternative is a decided sentence that the choice is deliberately per-law,
keyed on the member set, closing the question without a new class. Confident recommendation, not an ask: the
open-vs-closed keying is derivable from the laws as they stand [INV-121], so no human fork is owed on the
concept; only whether to declare it and in what words is open.
