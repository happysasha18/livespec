## F1 — The monthly archive file the law demands contradicts the day-dated file the existing machinery produces, and the conversion's repoint list is silent on the change

> "The system *shall* gather one calendar month's moved rows in one archive file and *shall* record one
> manifest line per archive file above the body." — R287.2 (PRODUCT_SPEC.md:6585); and "grow its manifest
> one line per monthly archive file" — R245.5 (PRODUCT_SPEC.md:5547); and the glossary: "one archive file
> gathers one calendar month's moved rows" — "queue archive" (PRODUCT_SPEC.md:180).

The law is monthly: one archive file per calendar month, one manifest line per file, a row moving in each
closing commit (R287.1). The existing machinery is day-dated: `scripts/rotate-doc.py` hard-writes
`docs/queue-archive/rotated-ROADMAP-%s.md % date` with `--date` defaulting to today (rotate-doc.py:137,141),
ARCHITECTURE.md's INV-209 cell names the archive `rotated-<doc>-<date>.md`, and the live tree holds
`rotated-ROADMAP-2026-07-18.md` — a full date, not a month. Under a per-closing-commit cadence, each
landing across a month would mint a fresh day-file and a fresh manifest line, which is exactly the
"one line per landing" shape R245.5 and roadmap-format.md line 29 say the queue must NOT have. The
conversion's consumer-repoint paragraph says only that `scripts/rotate-doc.py` "keeps reading the status
from cell index 3" (docs/roadmap-format.md:68) — it never names the two changes the monthly law forces:
(a) the archive filename moves from day to `YYYY-MM`, and (b) rotate-doc must APPEND a single row into the
existing month file and UPDATE that month's one manifest line, rather than create a new file per run.

Consequence: a maintainer landing a wish after conversion runs the unchanged rotate-doc, gets a new
`rotated-ROADMAP-2026-08-04.md` and a new manifest line, and by the second landing that month the queue
already violates R287.2 — while `guardrails/check-doc-rotation.py` (cross-checking manifest vs archive)
stays green because each file still matches its line. The law is broken silently.

Add to docs/roadmap-format.md's conversion section the rotate-doc changes as a named delta: the archive
file is named by year and month, rotate-doc appends the closing row into the current month's file and
edits that file's single manifest line, and check-doc-rotation reads one manifest line covering a
row-set that grows across commits. Name it must-hold at conversion (INV-39, green suite on the merged tree).

`defect · direct-contradiction (contradiction)`

## F2 — The mandated status and provenance dates in the body collide with the family no-history law's mechanical gate

> "A row's status cell *shall* carry one word of the closed status vocabulary … each set in lowercase
> italics and carrying its date" — R288.2 (PRODUCT_SPEC.md:6605); and "The provenance is whose word asked
> for the wish and the date … carried on the row" — docs/roadmap-format.md:50; against "The queue *shall*
> be written in the requirements format … inheriting … the no-history law … and *shall* restate none of
> them." — R286.1 (PRODUCT_SPEC.md:6565).

The queue inherits the family no-history law, whose mechanical enforcer is `guardrails/check-no-history.py`
(INV-253): it reds any ISO date, month-and-number, or bare year in a document body (check-no-history.py:44–46).
Yet R288.2 requires a date on EVERY live status cell and roadmap-format.md:50 requires a provenance date on
every wish cell. The two cannot both hold if that gate is pointed at ROADMAP. Today they do not collide only
because `test_no_history.py` asserts the gate is NOT wired into pre-push or CI
(`assertNotIn("check-no-history", …)`, test_no_history.py:42–44) — it scans the spec alone. The reconciliation
is real and correct (a live wish's origin date is live metadata; only FINISHED-work history moves out) and
is stated in prose — "the body stays the live queue, and the record of finished work moves out to the
archive" (docs/roadmap-format.md:29) — but R286.1's flat "inheriting the no-history law and restating none
of them" hides that the queue enforces no-history by a DIFFERENT net (the live-body law INV-276 + the
doc-rotation gate INV-209), not by check-no-history. A later maintainer "completing the family gate set"
who wires check-no-history to ROADMAP.md would red every mandated status and provenance date.

State, in the conversion section or under R287, that the queue's no-history net is the live-body law and the
doc-rotation gate, and that `check-no-history.py` is deliberately NOT pointed at ROADMAP.md because the body
carries live status and provenance dates by design — so the negative decision is on record and holds against
a future family-parity sweep.

`defect · internal-conflict (consistency)`

## F4 — The landing-map re-key "a body row left the body" cannot tell a delivery from a decline, and the architecture text is stale against R257

> "*when* a commit's diff moves a roadmap row from the body to the archive with its status naming *landed*,
> the system *shall* require the same commit to touch `NEXT_STEPS.md`" — R257.1 (PRODUCT_SPEC.md:5823); and
> "*shall* leave … a row closed to *declined*, *deferred*, or *superseded* … owing no refresh." — R257.3
> (PRODUCT_SPEC.md:5828); against the re-key "re-keys from 'a Status cell flipped to landed' to 'a body row
> left the body'" — docs/roadmap-format.md:68.

R257.1 keys the NEXT_STEPS demand on a row moving out with its status naming *landed*; R257.3 exempts a row
moving out as *declined* or *superseded* (which also leave the body, R287.1). The re-key phrasing "a body
row left the body" collapses that distinction: a declined row leaving the body would trigger the refresh
demand R257.3 says it does not owe. Because landed rows now live in the archive, the gate
(`guardrails/check-landing-next-steps.py`, which today reads the status from body cell index 3,
check-landing-next-steps.py:79,103) must read the moved row's status FROM the archive to know it landed. Two
holes stand: the re-key description drops the landed-vs-declined test, and ARCHITECTURE.md's INV-242 cell
still reads "a landing is a commit whose diff flips a ROADMAP.md row's status cell to `landed`" — a trigger
that no longer occurs post-conversion, contradicting R257.1.

Sharpen the re-key in docs/roadmap-format.md to "a body row moved to the archive whose archived status reads
*landed*" and add the archive-read as the gate's new source; and update ARCHITECTURE.md's INV-242 cell text
to the moved-to-archive trigger in the same delivery, so the architecture and R257 read the same law.

`defect · undefined-path (transitions)`

## F5 — "Terminal exit" for a multi-leg row is defined only in the definition page, not in the spec requirement that acts on it

> "*when* a row reaches a terminal exit — *landed*, *declined*, or *superseded* — the system *shall* move it
> verbatim … in the same commit that closes it." — R287.1 (PRODUCT_SPEC.md:6584), anchored [INV-276] only;
> against "A landing that leaves any leg open keeps its row *in-work* with the open leg named … since a row
> closes only whole." — docs/roadmap-format.md:36 (the whole-close law, INV-26; glossary "leg",
> PRODUCT_SPEC.md:123).

The multi-leg carve-out — a partial landing does NOT move the row, because it stays *in-work* until every
leg closes — is the composition point between R287 (terminal exit moves the row) and the whole-close law
(INV-26). It is stated in roadmap-format.md's status vocabulary and in the glossary "leg", but R287 itself
cites neither INV-26 nor the whole-close rule. A maintainer reading R287.1 alone, on a row with three legs
of which one just shipped, has textual license to move the row to the archive on the first leg's landing —
losing the two open legs from the live queue. The spec body owes the reference so "terminal" is unambiguous
where the acting requirement lives.

Add to R287.1 (or a new criterion in R287) the clause that a multi-leg row reaches a terminal exit only when
every leg is closed, anchored [INV-26], so the whole-close law composes with the move at the requirement
that performs it.

`defect · missing-rule (invariant)`

## F6 — A stranded `in-work` row has no defined re-read path

> "*in-work* — the wish is claimed by a session." — docs/roadmap-format.md:36; against the two parked
> statuses that DO carry a re-read: "*deferred* … re-read at the next milestone review and whenever a
> session takes its next wish" and "*far* … re-read at the milestone reviews" (docs/roadmap-format.md:37–38),
> and R287.4 re-reading deferred/far at queue-take and milestone [INV-129].

*deferred* and *far* rows each carry a stated re-read cadence; *in-work* does not. A session that claims a
row and then dies (a wipe, a crash, an abandoned window) leaves the row *in-work* with no revisit trigger and
no sweep that re-reads it — it is neither runnable-head material (it looks claimed) nor swept back. The queue
has no stated liveness guarantee that a claimed-then-abandoned row returns to runnable. This is the queue's
version of a stuck state.

State the re-reader for a stale *in-work* row: either the queue-take scan re-reads *in-work* rows against
live session records (an *in-work* row whose claiming session is no longer live returns to *queued*), or a
milestone sweep does. Name the actor and the trigger; where only the human can judge a stalled claim, this is
the very question ROADMAP row 411 (the far-tier "who took a thing and did not do it") already parks —
cross-reference it.

`defect · stuck-state (liveness)`
