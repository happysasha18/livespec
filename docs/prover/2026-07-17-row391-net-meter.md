# Prover — row 391, the net-liveness meter law (2026-07-17, short form per INV-61)

Small delta (kind: infra; footprint: single-module — a new self-contained shared shape plus its spec law
and the standard derivation-chain doc updates; architecture = new invariant INV-202, owner node guardrails).

- **Previous records:** red-proof-2026-07-17-row369.txt and 2026-07-17-muted-launch-pipefail.txt clean;
  no unfolded rows carried forward.
- **The delta in one line:** every net — a hook or a guard that watches for something — records its own
  two numbers, how often it RAN and how often it FIRED, and a silent net is read rather than trusted; the
  shared shape `guardrails/net_meter.py` (the pack-side arm of the personal layer's `hook-meter.py`) is a
  transparent wrapper logging one JSON line per invocation to `.live-spec/net-meter.jsonl` plus a `--report`
  reader that aggregates runs and fires against the host's declared roster and gives three readings — a
  zero-run roster net reds by name as a broken trigger (the roster being what lets a never-run net be named,
  since it writes no line), a net with runs at or over a declared window and zero fires is surfaced as a
  retirement candidate whose retirement is the human's call (never auto-retired, never auto-red for silence),
  everything firing reads live — spec clause INV-202 in the machines section + the Formal-index row,
  ARCHITECTURE.md guardrails owns-list + pin `guardrails/net_meter.py:1`, M-383, and thirteen tests
  red-proven against the pre-delta tree (the shared shape absent, the test module fails to load with
  FileNotFoundError; captured in red-proof-2026-07-17-row391.txt), then green.
- **Composition read:** no clash with the existing net laws. INV-41 (the number names its watcher) is the
  kin the clause cites — this law extends it, giving the nets themselves the watcher they lacked. The
  check-that-looked-at-nothing kin (ROADMAP row 384) is the same check measured over time rather than over
  an input set. The meter never becomes a hard push gate in this landing: the shape and reader
  ship complete and usable on demand the way the personal `hook-meter.py` is, and a host reads its own nets
  against its declared roster and decides for itself whether a zero-run net's red also stops its push — so
  live-spec's own push is not reddened by a not-yet-populated roster, and no planned-item `[target]` is owed.
  The `--report` reader's own exit-non-zero-on-a-zero-run-net is the delivered mechanical guarantee, proven
  by test, independent of any host push wiring.
- **Reference-implementation fidelity:** the pack-side shape keeps the personal `hook-meter.py`'s transparent
  contract (stdin through, stdout re-emitted, the net's own exit code preserved, a failed log swallowed) and
  the same three readings, generalizing "hook" to "net" (a guard fires on a non-zero exit as well as a
  block-decision JSON) and adding the declared roster (per-net zero-run detection, absent from the log-only
  personal reader) and the declared window (silence reads as retirement only at or over it).
- **Verdict:** ready to ship — 0 must-fix, 0 should-clarify.
