## What changed, and why the law is intact

**Caps-shout (95 → 0).** Every ALL-CAPS emphasis word was judged one of two ways:

- A defined closed vocabulary or proper name → allowlisted in `spec-style-lint.py`, left uppercase
  (it is a literal term, not shout): the design-review loop rest-states CONVERGES / WAITS / STANDS DOWN
  [INV-154], the push-gate reach-map categories EXPLICIT / CONSERVATIVE / SELF-TESTED [INV-45], and the
  prior-art framework name BMAD. Each was verified to occur only in its defining clause.
- Plain emphasis → lowercased in place (register R12: the statement carries the force). No obligation,
  quantifier, modality, or scope moved — only the casing. The change was applied by a per-line,
  per-token script bounded exactly to the linter's own token boundary, then verified by the full suite
  (which pins many spec phrases as exact strings) and spec-freeze (numbers, paths, anchors, markers).

Where the full suite pinned a lowercased word, the needle was re-cased to the plain form — the same law,
de-emphasised — and logged in `docs/restyle-repoint-log.md`: test_detached_work_visibility (start line /
done digest), test_docs_layout_vehicle (multiset check and), test_made_with_attribution (an offer, never
a gate — spec + publish SKILL), test_scenario_entry_exit (scenario level), test_traceability (the
project's kind proposes the dimensions; accumulate on one page).

**Scissors (5 → 0).** Each banned contrast frame ("X, not Y" / "— never Y") became its own positive
sentence with the boundary preserved as a plain statement. The load-bearing case: INV-137's canonical
rule name was itself a scissors frame, "the orchestrator reads to decide, not to discover". It was
renamed to the positive "the orchestrator reads to decide; discovery reads go to workers" across every
LIVING home — the spec prose clause, base rule 25 (live-spec-base SKILL), the docs/lenses.md heading,
and the three test needles. The dated records that fixed the name at landing time are not rewritten:
JOURNAL.md, docs/prover/2026-07-13-gap0-read-discipline.md, and the landed ROADMAP row 301 (archived,
not reworded). The rule's obligations are unchanged: the lead reads only to decide or cite, and
dispatches reads done to discover or understand at scale.

**Second-person (6 → 0).** Reader-addressed "you"/"your" in the spec's own prose became named actors
(the human, the host). The onboarding scenario heading "Meeting your settings" was renamed to "Meeting
the settings" (its [feature: F-onboarding] tag intact; the two index home-cells updated to match). One
second-person survived deliberately: the read-contract literal "needs your word: what, by when", a line
the product shows the human. The linter now exempts a second-person word wholly inside a double-quoted
span — a cited product-copy literal is the product's voice, not the spec's register. Exactly one such
span exists in PRODUCT_SPEC.md, so the exemption is precisely targeted.

**Reassurance (4 → 0), machine-jargon (2 → 0), negation-opener (1 → 0), future-narration (1 → 0).**
Bare "simply" dropped; "questionnaire" → "round of questions" / "asks nothing"; the remote-arm block
re-opened with what it is ("the inbox also opens to a seat that shares no filesystem") rather than what
it is not; "it will hold the session" → present tense "it holds the session".
