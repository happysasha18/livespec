## What I tried hardest to break (and could not turn into a defect)

- **Does adding a flake to the pinned skip-set violate "the skip-set is exactly the expected pinned list"?**
  No direct violation: the quarantine becomes part of the EXPECTED list, so `skip-set == expected` still
  holds mechanically. The weakness is distinguishability and staleness, not a contradiction — filed as R1.
- **Does INV-155's "no retry" contradict INV-23's "the session retries and moves on"?** Not head-on: INV-23's
  own walk (line 1030) already says the WATCHED line "replaces the silent retry", so neither lane truly
  licenses a retry once you read the walk. The live gap is the ambiguous EXAMPLE at 1015 and the classification
  seam (D1/D2), not a bare contradiction of the retry ban.

## Strongest finding (two sentences)

A browser/CDP-timing flake is an own test flaking on the external harness, so it satisfies BOTH INV-155's
own-test roots ("a timing ... assumption", "a network or filesystem race" — fix it) AND INV-23's external-harness
noise ("a separate home" — WATCHED and move on), and the stated "own vs external" boundary gives no tie-breaker.
The misroute has teeth: an own flake sent to the ledger can be dated into an AGREED NON-PROBLEM [INV-9] — the
permanent toleration INV-155 exists to forbid — so the clause needs the removable-where disambiguator on both sides.
