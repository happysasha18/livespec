Образец A — «Утверждённый прототип — эталон вида»

Как сейчас в спеке (плотная проза с историей внутри правила):

An approved look lives in its artifact — the clause that encodes it points there, and the build looks at it. Text cannot carry a feel: a spec clause born of an approved visual prototype was once rebuilt from its own prose and shipped a cheap look-alike — 75 tests green, because tests derived from a misread spec prove the misreading (tlvphoto's door and gallery, 2026-07-05). So when the human approves a sketch as the look, the prototype becomes the norm for look and feel, and one law with four arms guards it…

Раунд 3 — жанр юзкейса:

Feature: An approved prototype is the design reference

User Story: As the product owner, I want the prototype I approved — not the prose describing it — to be what the feature is built and judged against, so that what ships looks like what I said yes to.

You look at a rendered sketch and say "yes — this is the door." From that word on, the sketch itself carries the look: the spec points at a frozen copy of it, the builder opens it before writing a line, and review compares the result to it — never to anyone's reading of the text.

Acceptance Criteria:

  1. WHEN the owner approves a prototype as the look THEN a copy SHALL be frozen under docs/norms/ with a short note: what it is, when it was approved, which sketch it came from.
  2. WHEN a requirement is born from an approved prototype THEN the requirement SHALL end with a pointer to the frozen copy (norm: docs/norms/door.html) — never to a working sketch, which may still change or be deleted.
  3. WHEN a builder picks up such a requirement THEN they SHALL open the reference before building and record one line: how the plan differs from it.
  4. IF the reference was never opened THEN review SHALL reject the work; the final check SHALL judge look and feel against the same reference.
  5. WHILE a "mockup first" entry condition stands on a task, a general "go ahead and build" SHALL NOT cancel it — only an explicit cancellation naming it SHALL.

Why this exists: a gallery was once rebuilt from its text description alone, without opening the approved prototype. All 75 tests passed — they had been derived from the same misread text — and what shipped looked nothing like what was approved. (tlvphoto, 2026-07-05) [INV-43]

Образец B — «Баг режет очередь»

Как сейчас в спеке:

A bug may interrupt the wish in-work. The interrupted wish moves to parked: a checkpoint is written (failing test names if red, hypothesis, touched files — nothing red is ever committed), the bug takes the lane, and the parked wish resumes as the immediate next landing — ahead of ANY queued wish, a quick win included: a bubble [T-11] jumps only fresh queued wishes, never a resume…

Раунд 3 — жанр юзкейса:

Feature: A bug takes the lane; parked work returns first

User Story: As the product owner, I want a bug in the shipped product fixed ahead of everything, without losing the feature that was mid-build, so that the product heals immediately and no work evaporates.

Mid-feature, you report: "the card is broken on the phone." The feature parks at a checkpoint, the bug takes the lane, and once the last waiting bug is fixed, the parked feature comes back first — ahead of the whole queue.

Acceptance Criteria:

  1. WHEN a bug report arrives while a feature is mid-build THEN the feature SHALL park with a checkpoint — failing test names, the current hypothesis, touched files — and red work SHALL NOT be committed.
  2. WHILE a bug is being fixed, nothing SHALL interrupt it — a newly arriving bug waits, whatever its priority.
  3. WHEN several bugs wait THEN they SHALL queue critical-first; equals order by arrival.
  4. WHEN no bug is waiting THEN parked features SHALL resume ahead of the entire regular queue — nothing jumps a resume, quick wins included.
  5. IF several lanes were parked at once THEN each SHALL resume from its own checkpoint, in the order they were due to land.

At most one feature is parked per lane. [T-9, T-11, T-18]

Вопрос 2 — строгость критериев

Критерии приёмки можно писать двумя голосами. Строгий (как в образцах выше, стиль Kiro): «WHEN a bug arrives THEN the feature SHALL park…» — однозначно и проверяемо машиной, но звучит формально. Обычный (стиль BMAD): «A bug arriving parks the feature at a checkpoint; red work is never committed.» — читается легче, проверяемость та же, пока каждый пункт несёт одно поведение.
