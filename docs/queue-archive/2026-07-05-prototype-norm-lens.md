# Wish: visual clauses carry their prototype pointer; the build step opens it (from tlvphoto, 2026-07-05; was mis-dated 07-06 at intake — invented-time family, corrected against the clock session 10)

Incident (tlvphoto, door+gallery): a spec clause born from an APPROVED visual prototype was rebuilt from
the clause's TEXT alone — the prototype ("что ближе сейчас" ask line; the Room museum hang confirmed as
the gallery) was never reopened, and the build shipped a cheap look-alike. The suite (75 green) proved
the misreading perfectly: tests derived from a misread spec cannot catch the misreading. Alexander
caught it by eye next morning ("на двери нет вопроса; после двери нет галереи").

Proposed pack rules:
1. **spec-author:** when a clause encodes an approved visual prototype, the clause CITES the artifact
   path (a "norm: <file>" pointer) — the prose carries the laws, the artifact stays the norm for look
   and feel.
2. **build-pipeline (code step):** before building a surface whose clauses carry a norm-pointer, OPEN
   the artifact and record a one-line diff of plan vs prototype; a missing check is a defect at review.
3. **build-pipeline (door step):** a story's declared ENTRY CONDITION (mockup-first / show-first) is not
   cancellable by a general "go build" — only by the human naming it.
4. **product-prover:** a lens — for every visual clause, does a prototype/artifact back it, and does the
   clause's text contradict its own artifact (the "wordless ≠ no question" class)?
