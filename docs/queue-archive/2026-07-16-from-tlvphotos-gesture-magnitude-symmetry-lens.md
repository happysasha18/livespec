# Wish: the paired-transition lens should ask about MAGNITUDE, not only existence (incident-keyed)

From: the tlvphotos window, 2026-07-16.

## The incident

The owner reported on his own device that closing the inspect zoom by pinch demanded a much deeper
squeeze (commit at ~0.82× of resting) than the spread that opened it (any spread past 1×) — the
reverse gesture existed but asked a different magnitude, so the close felt like "an extra pinch".
The spec stated the reverse close exists, so the paired-transition reversibility lens (INV-126
family) was satisfied and every prover pass over the zoom section came back clean. The owner's own
read, paraphrased: this is a prover-class miss, and it slipped because the detail was never a spec
sentence.

## The gap, named

The paired-transition symmetry lens asks two questions today: does the opposite direction have a
stated transition, and does the opening gesture's inverse stand among the ways to close. Neither
asks whether the inverse demands the SAME MAGNITUDE as the forward move. Where a pair rides a
continuous quantity (a pinch span, a drag distance, a wheel accumulation), the two directions can
both exist and still be asymmetric in what they cost the hand.

## The proposed extension

Add a third sub-question to the paired-transition lens: where a paired open/close rides a
continuous, reversible quantity, the spec owes a sentence on whether the two directions demand the
same magnitude — symmetric, or a named deliberate asymmetry — decided or `[default]`-tagged like
any facet, with motion feel staying the human's gate. The spec-author facet list's
paired-transition entry grows the same half, so the author writes the sentence before the prover
ever looks. Incident above is the entry's key, per the curated-list law.
