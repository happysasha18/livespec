# Wish: the prover must sweep CROSS-CUTTING requirements across every surface

**The item.** Teach product-prover a standing check for cross-cutting requirements — laws that must
ride EVERY feature, not one section. Measurement is the worked example: "every visitor-facing event
emits an analytics beat" is a single law, but its proof is a sweep over ALL surfaces and transitions.
The prover today reviews each feature's section in isolation; nobody asks "does the measurement law
reach this new surface?", so coverage holes accumulate silently.

**Why (today's primary source, tlvphoto 2026-07-10 morning).** Alexander had to notice HIMSELF that
the site's analytics cover only some beats (the walk unfold/exit pair, the quiz stages) while whole
surfaces emit nothing: photograph downloads, the side room with the polaroids. His words: statistics
should ride all events, some requests must run consistently through the WHOLE functionality, and the
prover should have prescribed this itself — he is surprised it never found the gap. A second data
point the same morning, same shape: appending five works («open more») does not refresh the told
story — a composition no section owns, so no section's review could fail it.

**Suggested mechanism (the pack's call how).** The spec names its cross-cutting laws in one place
(measurement, localization, accessibility, error handling — whatever the product declares). The
prover walk gains one station: for each declared cross-cutting law, enumerate every entity, surface,
and transition in the spec and demand the law's clause or an explicit dated exemption per item. A
missing clause is a finding, same rank as a broken invariant. The spec-author side owns the twin
habit: a NEW surface's section must state its line against each declared cross-cutting law before
the prover ever sees it.

**Who throws it.** The tlvphoto window, on Alexander's direct word (2026-07-10 ~10:38).
