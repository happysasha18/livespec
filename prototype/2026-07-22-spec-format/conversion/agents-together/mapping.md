## 2a. Vocabulary decisions

| Source wording | Decision |
|---|---|
| "cadence" used for two rhythms — the producer's regeneration number (`INV-186`) and, loosely, a receiver's inbox-check rhythm ("a one-shot check that reads such a deposit at its own cadence", `INV-236`) | The glossary term **cadence** is kept for the producer-owned number alone; the receiver's use is reworded as plain language in R2.7 ("on the receiver's own rhythm, whenever it next runs"), so one domain noun carries one meaning. |
| "the pack answers who owns it" (`INV-197`) — an inert bundle cannot answer | R8.5 names the concrete actor the source supports: the pack is a host (`INV-97`) with its own inbox, swept by its assigned session (`T-10`, `INV-10`), so the answering actor is "the pack repo's own assigned session, sweeping that inbox". The source's own actorless wording is recorded as a finding in `GAPS.md` (G4). |
| the reserved source words | Spelled as the literal strings `from-owner` and `stranger-` in R7.1, per `inbox/README.md`. |
