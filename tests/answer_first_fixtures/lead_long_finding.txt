[16:55] The report was slow because it re-queried the database once per row instead of once per page.

Here is how I found it and what the fix looks like. I started from a cold load and captured a trace, which showed the total time dominated by database round-trips rather than rendering. Counting the queries, the page issued a little over four hundred of them for a single screen of results, one for each row's author lookup, which is the classic N-plus-one shape. Batching those lookups into a single query per page drops the count from four hundred to two and cuts the cold render from about six seconds to under one. I have staged the change with a test that drives a real page load and asserts the query count stays at two, so a future regression that reintroduces the per-row lookup fails the suite immediately.
